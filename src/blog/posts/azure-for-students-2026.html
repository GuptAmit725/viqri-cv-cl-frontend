How to Sign Up (It Takes 5 Minutes)

  1. Visit the Azure for Students page: Go to the official Microsoft Azure for Students website
  2. Click "Activate now": You'll be prompted to sign in with your school email
  3. Verify your academic status: Microsoft will verify you're a student through your educational email domain
  4. Complete your profile: Fill in basic information about your institution
  5. Start building: Your $100 credit is activated immediately

No credit card, no hidden fees, no catches. If Microsoft can't automatically verify your student status, you may need to provide additional documentation like a student ID.

Making the Most of Your $100 Credits

Your credits last 12 months, so here's how to maximize them:

Start Small and Scale

Don't deploy expensive virtual machines right away. Begin with free-tier services or small instances. Azure's pay-as-you-go model means you only pay when resources are running.

Use Free Services Strategically

Prioritize services that remain free even after your credits expire. This way, your projects can continue running beyond the 12-month period.

Monitor Your Spending

The Azure portal shows your credit balance and daily spending. Set up spending alerts to avoid accidentally exhausting your credits on a misconfigured service.

Shut Down When Not in Use

Virtual machines and databases charge by the hour. Stop them when you're not actively workingâ€”you can always restart them later.

Learn Cost Optimization

Understanding cloud cost management is a valuable career skill. Experiment with different service tiers, reserved instances, and spot pricing to learn what works best.
