Making Your Resume ATS-Friendly: What You Need to Know

Viqri Team
•
January 25, 2026
•
6 min read

You've crafted the perfect resume. Your achievements are impressive, your experience is relevant, and the design looks stunning. You hit submit and... crickets. What went wrong?

The harsh reality: your beautiful resume might never reach human eyes. Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) process over 98% of Fortune 500 applications, and if your resume isn't optimized for these digital gatekeepers, you're essentially invisible.

What is an ATS, Really?

An Applicant Tracking System is software that companies use to manage their recruitment process. Think of it as a filter that:

  • Parses and stores resume data in a database
  • Ranks candidates based on keywords and qualifications
  • Helps recruiters search and filter applications
  • Automatically rejects resumes that don't meet basic criteria

The problem? ATS software varies widely in sophistication. While some modern systems use AI and machine learning, many still rely on basic text parsing that struggles with creative formatting.

Warning: Even if you're perfectly qualified, poor ATS optimization can result in automatic rejection. Studies show that 75% of qualified candidates are filtered out before a human ever sees their application.

The ATS Compatibility Checklist

✓ Must-Have Elements

  • Standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills)
  • Chronological work history with clear dates
  • Keywords from the job description naturally incorporated
  • Simple, clean formatting with no graphics or images in body text
  • Standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, Georgia)
  • File format: .docx or PDF (check job posting for preference)

Formatting Rules That ATS Can't Handle

Avoid these common formatting mistakes that confuse ATS systems:

1. Complex Layouts

Tables, text boxes, columns, and headers/footers can cause parsing errors. ATS systems read left to right, top to bottom. Anything that disrupts this flow gets jumbled or ignored.

2. Graphics and Images

This includes photos, logos, charts, graphs, and decorative elements. ATS can't read images, so any text embedded in graphics is lost. Even your name, if it's in an image format, might not be captured.

3. Unusual Fonts and Formatting

Fancy fonts, excessive bolding, italics, underlines, or text colors can cause parsing problems. Stick to standard fonts in black text.

4. Special Characters and Symbols

Most ATS can handle standard bullets, but symbols like ☆ ♦ ☺ or creative Unicode characters may not parse correctly. Use simple round or square bullets.

The Power of Keywords

Keywords are the currency of ATS. Here's how to use them effectively:

Mining for Gold

Start with the job description. Identify:

  • Required skills: These are non-negotiable
  • Preferred qualifications: Include these when possible
  • Industry terminology: Use the same language as the posting
  • Certifications and credentials: Spell them out fully and use acronyms

Strategic Placement

Don't just list keywords—contextualize them:

Instead of: "Managed projects"
Write: "Managed cross-functional Agile projects using Scrum methodology, delivering 15+ software releases on schedule"

The Acronym Rule

Always spell out acronyms the first time, then use the acronym: "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)" not just "SEO". ATS may search for either version.

Section-by-Section ATS Optimization

Contact Information

Place this at the top, not in a header. Include:

  • Full name
  • Phone number (format doesn't usually matter, but (555) 123-4567 is safe)
  • Email address
  • LinkedIn URL (optional but recommended)
  • City and state (full addresses are outdated)

Work Experience

Use this format:

  • Company Name | Location
  • Job Title | Month Year – Month Year
  • Achievement-focused bullet points with keywords

Education

Include:

  • Degree type and major (spell out, e.g., "Bachelor of Science in Computer Science")
  • University name
  • Graduation date or expected graduation
  • GPA if above 3.5 and you're a recent graduate

Skills Section

This is keyword gold. Create categories:

  • Technical Skills: Programming languages, software, tools
  • Certifications: All relevant professional credentials
  • Languages: If applicable, with proficiency levels

Testing Your Resume

Before submitting, run these tests:

  1. Plain Text Test: Copy your resume into Notepad. If it's a jumbled mess, the ATS will struggle too.
  2. Keyword Analysis: Use free tools to compare your resume against the job description
  3. File Format Test: Save as both .docx and PDF, then open to verify formatting holds
  4. Mobile View: ATS aside, recruiters often review on phones. Ensure readability

Common Myths Debunked

Myth: More keywords = better results

Reality: Keyword stuffing is detectable and will hurt you. Use keywords naturally in context. Modern ATS can identify "keyword stuffing" patterns.

Myth: You need to exactly match every requirement

Reality: Many listings are wish lists. Focus on matching 70-80% of requirements. Quality keywords beat quantity.

Myth: All PDF resumes get rejected

Reality: Modern ATS can parse PDFs well. However, some older systems prefer .docx. When in doubt, check the job posting for guidance.

Advanced ATS Strategies

The Dual Resume Approach

Create two versions:

  1. ATS Version: Simple, keyword-rich, perfectly formatted
  2. Human Version: More design elements, for networking and direct submissions

Quantify Everything

ATS often ranks candidates by impact. Numbers make your achievements parseable and comparable:

  • "Increased sales" → "Increased sales by 35% ($2.4M) within 8 months"
  • "Led team" → "Led team of 12 developers across 3 time zones"
  • "Improved efficiency" → "Reduced processing time by 40%, saving 15 hours/week"

The Cover Letter Hack

When you can, submit a cover letter—even if optional. It's another opportunity to include keywords and context that might not fit naturally in your resume.

Staying Human While Beating the Bots

The goal isn't to game the system—it's to ensure your genuine qualifications get seen. An ATS-friendly resume should:

  • Be readable by both machines and humans
  • Showcase your actual accomplishments
  • Use industry-standard terminology
  • Tell a coherent career story

Remember: passing the ATS is just the first hurdle. Once your resume reaches a human, it needs to be compelling, clear, and professional.

The Bottom Line

In 2026, ATS optimization isn't optional—it's fundamental. But it doesn't mean sacrificing your unique value or professional identity. By understanding how these systems work and following proven formatting guidelines, you can ensure your qualifications get the attention they deserve.
